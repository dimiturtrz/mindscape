- 2026-07-07 — **NEURAL-SIGNAL SYNTHESIS (EEG/fNIRS) — DIFFUSION-FIRST, corrects the old GAN-only view.** The field moved GAN→diffusion→flow; diffusion matches/surpasses GANs in fidelity+coverage and is more stable (GANs unstable) — GAN (Ye 2021) is the DATED baseline. Time-series diffusion still maturing vs image/audio. **KEY find — SCDM (arXiv 2407.04736): a diffusion model that generates fNIRS FROM EEG (cross-modal), claiming "EEG + synthetic fNIRS ≈ or > EEG + real fNIRS".** Our reading: that CONFIRMS our redundancy/identity-trap finding — you can only regenerate fNIRS from EEG and match real if fNIRS carries ~no info EEG lacks; SCDM's win is convenience (skip fNIRS hardware), not added signal. Physics-forward path: 2026 High-Fidelity 3D fNIRS sim (2605.30552, mesh Monte-Carlo, parameterized HRF+systemic+artifacts, fNIRS-only, CC-BY; canonical-HRF = edge-of-scope for continuous n-back) + 2024 precursor (2405.11242) + SEREEGA (EEG). **Verdict: synthesis adds variety, not discriminative signal — no generator manufactures the 2-vs-3 signal. - 2026-07-03 — **SHIN 2018 N-BACK DATASET DEEP-DIVE: RAW FACTS FOR METHOD SELECTION** — Shin 2018 (n=26, 0/2/3-back, 234 trials/class); BenchNIRS cross-subject 5-fold baseline: LDA **38.9%**, CNN **39.3%**, LSTM **34.4%** (all vs 33.3% chance, only LDA/CNN significant) [S14]. Within-subject shrinkage-LDA 66% (−27 pts gap to cross-subject) [S16]. Time-axis treatment: classical mean/slope/peak features → LDA (39% cross-subject honest); raw time series CNN-LSTM (78–92% reported but mostly within-subject or different tasks, no Shin 2018 cross-subject DL winner exists) [S17-S19]. Sample-size reality: fNIRS DL overfits n<30 without regularization; LSTM underperforms LDA on Shin (34.4% < 38.9%); random-split CV inflates by 25–40 pts vs true LOSO; BenchNIRS consensus = LDA wins cross-subject, DL requires data augmentation to compete on small N [S14, S20-S22]. Recent SOTA (2023-2025): fNIRS-T 78.22% (different task, not Shin 2018), fNIRSNet 6.58% gain (within-subject only), CNN-LSTM 88.69% (pilot workload, not n-back) [S19, S23-S24]. **VERDICT:** No published cross-subject fNIRS DL method beats Shrinkage-LDA (38–40%) on Shin 2018 n-back yet. Prioritize: (1) Shrinkage-LDA baseline, (2) domain adaptation if <45%, (3) fNIRS-T with rigorous LOSO validation if baseline sufficient. Do NOT expect >50% cross-subject without adaptation/transfer. → **`deep_dives/2026-07-01_fnirs_decoding_sota.md` [UPDATE 2026-07-03]** [PARTIAL — SOTA on Shin clear, time-axis treatment & sample-size limits documented, no clear DL winner]
